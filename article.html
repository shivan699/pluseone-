Understanding CGHS Coverage: A Complete Guide for Government Employees

AV
Dr. Aisha Verma Healthcare Policy Analyst
📅 12 Jul 2026 ⏱ – min read
hero-cghs.jpg

What is CGHS?

The Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS) is a comprehensive healthcare programme designed to provide medical care to central government employees, pensioners, and their eligible dependents. Since its inception, CGHS has grown into one of the largest health assurance schemes in the country, covering both outpatient and inpatient treatment across a wide network of dispensaries and empanelled hospitals.

Unlike private insurance, CGHS is not a reimbursement-only model — beneficiaries can access cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals for most major procedures, provided the correct referral and approval process is followed.

"CGHS was built on a simple idea: healthcare access shouldn't depend on how much paperwork you're willing to do after you're already unwell." — Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Annual Report

Who is Eligible?

Eligibility under CGHS is fairly broad, but it's worth checking your exact category before you assume coverage. Generally, the scheme covers:

  • Serving central government employees and their dependent family members
  • Central government pensioners and their eligible dependents
  • Members of Parliament and ex-Members of Parliament
  • Freedom fighters and certain autonomous body employees, where notified
  • Accredited journalists in specific CGHS-covered cities
💡
Good to knowDependent parents, spouse, and unmarried children (subject to income criteria) are usually covered under the same CGHS card as the primary beneficiary.

What Does It Cover?

CGHS coverage spans a wide range of services — from routine OPD consultations to major surgical procedures. The table below breaks down the broad coverage categories and how they're typically processed.

CategoryCoverage TypeApproval Needed
OPD ConsultationCashless at CGHS dispensaryNo
Diagnostic TestsCashless / ReimbursementSometimes
Planned SurgeryCashless at empanelled hospitalYes — prior permission
Emergency SurgeryCashless or reimbursementPost-facto intimation
MedicinesCashless via CGHS pharmacyNo
⚠️
Common mistakeSkipping prior permission for planned, non-emergency surgeries is the single biggest reason CGHS reimbursement claims get delayed or rejected.

How to Apply for a CGHS Card

The application process has become significantly simpler with the online CGHS portal. Here's the general flow most beneficiaries follow:

  • Fill out the online CGHS beneficiary application form with your service or pension details
  • Upload identity proof, address proof, and a recent photograph
  • Pay the applicable subscription based on your pay level (one-time or annual, depending on your category)
  • Submit the form through your department's nodal officer for verification
  • Receive your CGHS plastic card, usually within a few weeks of approval
✅
TipYou can start using CGHS services with your acknowledgement receipt while your physical card is being processed — most dispensaries accept this as valid proof.

Finding an Empanelled Hospital

Before any planned treatment, it's worth confirming that your chosen hospital is currently empanelled under CGHS for your city — empanelment lists are reviewed periodically, and hospitals occasionally move on or off the list. The official CGHS website maintains a searchable, city-wise list that's updated regularly.
